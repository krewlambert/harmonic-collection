CLIFF GANT

"the body can be a home"

the world sprinted out
of death

and the sky opened up

big and gorgeous

spilling into me until

I was

so very tiny and

outside of myself

a promise of birds

streaked across heaven’s

underbelly

the body can be a home

and nothing can touch me

Do you remember how much fun we had
playing in the bellies of beached whales?
during early march whole pods would wash ashore millions incalculable
Do you remember how cold the water was then? we used to knock our fists along their chests
until we found we were running out of rib
and you would find the perfect spot to cut us-sized holes right below the sternum
With that big knife you stole from your dad while he slept his thumbs
without halfmoon—
Just in case you told me
Though I only ever saw you use it to open portals
Do you remember how bright it was in those heavy bodies?


I’ve been told about a hill
covered with thousands of unbroken, wild ponies
I have yet to find it
God knows I’ve searched
Maybe I’ve passed it on the highway without knowing,
red knuckled
With the calm of steer
We tend to limp around the nucleus
Holding on to dog-toothed marginalia
sprawled across
sidewalk chalk residuals dissolved,
wet with sky

Cliff Gant (b. 2001) is a NYC-based writer who graduated from UCLA (class of 2024).
Cliff is a dear friend of Petal Books and will be the inaugural artist featured in our writing series.
Featured above are two recent poems, with backing visuals created by Shane Dillon for the "everaftermemory" series.
